Population Density in the United States (2000)

by Calvin Lu


Raster data visualization on population density across the United States.


The project will illustrate the population density in states across the Continental United States. Population density influences various socio-economic factors and public policies such as education districts and federal funding. By illustrating population density across the country, the visualizations will display patterns between population density, borders, cities, and capitals. In many cases, the state's capital is located in a major metropolitan area. This project will generate a deeper understanding of population density and spark new inquiries for implications concerning urbanization and overcrowding.


I hypothesize that cities with the highest population densities will be near the state's capital.


We will be analyzing the population density across the United States. The data driving this graph is sourced from the NASA's Socio-Economic Data and Applications Center on U.S. Census Grids in 2000. The visualizations on the right display the population density. Use the selectors to overlay various other data and the color drop down menu to select a day/night color scheme.
